WHAT IS “BETWEEN HERCULES AND A BUTTERFLY”?

PARIS catacombs

Between Hercules and a Butterfly is a unique series of exhibitions about people who were forced to leave their homelands. The first exhibition was held in the Parisian Catacombs, the second under a bridge in Vienna, and the third in a 24-hour kiosk in Berlin.

VIENNA Under the bridge

This project aims to support immigrants and convey their experiences to a European audience. The unusual venues were chosen deliberately, each representing a specific aspect of immigrant life.

BERLIN späti

Immigrants must adapt to the new reality of living in a new place. They are neither fully in their homeland nor completely settled in their new home. To survive, they must perform feats akin to Hercules while also being as vulnerable as a newly emerged butterfly. This state can be described as being “Between Hercules and a Butterfly.”

NEW-YORK Carousel

The project’s logo features two types of quotation marks that are used in different parts of the world, symbolizing the dual existence of immigrants. They retain their native culture while being quoted by a new one. They face the depression of losing their friends and home while navigating new chal- lenges everyday. Exhausted, they struggle with daily tasks but must continue to work and adapt to the unfamiliar language of their new life.

“«BETWEEN HERCULES AND A BUTTERFLY»” PARISIAN CATACOMBS

Each of the 16 participants, whether Ukrainian, Russian or from Kazakhastan, has experienced the hardships of immigration.

There is no better place to convey the borderline state of immigrant life as underground tunnels, sewers, catacombs. The underground world of Paris is a point between life and no life. Feeling undead, this border state resembles where we find ourselves once we move to a new country. Catacombs, technical rooms or sewers are hidden from everyday life. The difficulties of immigrant life are hidden in the same way.

«CRYSTAL CAVE» BY MISHA GUDWIN

What is my role ?

I’m not a traditional curator, that’s why I call myself
a director.

I organize not just an event, but more like a ritual.
My responsibility is to invent the form and the concept of the exhibition, find a place and artists, invite audience and journalists, look for photographers and videographers, make posters and presentations, and install everything by myself including lights and works. After all that I write text as an allergic reaction to what has happened.

And all that works is only for not more than 2 hours of the show like a film duration

«SUPER SEXY JUICY FLOWERS» BY JENIA FILATOVA

For me it is important to have a vertical system of interaction with artists or institutions.

I don’t select or reject artists, I take any author who wants to speak up. It means a world to
me first of all to give people the opportunity to tell their story. I don’t care about the status of this human being, whether that person is a professional artist or just a beginner, at my exhibitions all stories are equal.

2023

«THANK YOU, MY LAND. YOU ARE LIKE MADNESS» BY ALINA IZMAYLOVA

2023

Each work in the catacombs acquired a new additional meaning.
For example, architect Boris Bernaskoni’s small ceramic house in the endless space of the catacombs became an image of a lost home, the work is called a house for bedbugs, an ironic name for the time (it was a period of bedbug infestation in Paris, which coincided with the moment of mass immigration of people from Ukraine and Russia). As well as a video art by Jenia Filatova with flower-people, which by nature are very glossy and perfect in this case were projected onto the ceiling and merged with the expressive texture of the place, perfect skin covered with cracks and began to ossify.

If making of this exhibition felt like a wild venture, the exhibition itself looked like a ritual. It was wonderful to see how the eyes of the people and artists who came were lit up.

Despite the fact that the sewer manhole that served as the entrance to the exhibition was open the whole time, the police did not arrive.

Thus began the series of exhibitions ‘Between Hercules and the Butterfly’.
